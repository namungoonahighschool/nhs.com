Preamble & Institutional Charter

We, the students of Namungoona High School, commit ourselves to discipline, respect, responsibility, and academic excellence. These articles guide conduct, and any violation shall attract corrective disciplinary action aimed at building character.

Our Mission

To provide quality education in a disciplined environment, nurturing responsible citizens who strive to excel and contribute positively to society.

Our Vision

To be a centre of academic excellence and character development, recognized for shaping future leaders with integrity, innovation, and purpose.

Our Discipline Code (Core Values & Rules)

  • We respect ourselves, others, and school property.
  • We are punctual, prepared, and committed to learning.
  • We Uphold honesty, integrity, and responsibility in all we do.
  • We maintain a clean, safe, and orderly environment.
  • We follow school rules and accept consequences.
  • We reject corruption, bullying, and all forms of misconduct.
  • We wear our uniform with pride and act as ambassadors of the school.

CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PRINCIPLES

Article 1: Core Values Expansion

  • Discipline: Ensures students follow rules, manage behavior, and remain focused on goals, creating an environment where learning thrives.
  • Respect: Promotes positive relationships among students, teachers, and leaders, encouraging understanding, tolerance, and proper conduct.
  • Integrity: Requires honesty and moral uprightness, ensuring students act truthfully and uphold ethical standards.
  • Responsibility: Ensures students are accountable for their actions, duties, and choices.
  • Justice: Ensures fairness in all decisions, guaranteeing equal treatment and unbiased judgment.

CHAPTER TWO: STRUCTURE OF THE SCHOOL COURT

To systematically manage discipline, the school institutes a formal judicial branch consisting of specialized desks:

  • Chief Judge
  • Deputy Chief Judge
  • Prosecutor / Attorney General
  • Court Clerk
  • Deputy Court Clerk
  • The Intelligence Desk System
  • Advocates / Defense Lawyers
  • Court Spokesperson

CHAPTER THREE: DETAILED ROLES OF COURT OFFICIALS

Article 3: The Chief Judge

Presides over all court sessions, reviews complex case files, maintains strict order within hearings, delivers final fair judgments based on facts, and oversees the overall execution of duties by other court officials.

Article 4: The Deputy Chief Judge

Assists the Chief Judge in organizing legal files, supervises the general professional conduct of court members, and steps in to preside over sessions when the Chief Judge is unavailable.

Article 5: The Prosecutor / Attorney General

The Prosecutor represents the school in legal cases, presenting charges against accused individuals and ensuring justice through proper legal procedures.

Article 6: The Court Clerk & Deputy Court Clerk

The Court Clerk coordinates records, manages safety structures for court archives, and tracks timeline metrics securely.

Article 7: The Intelligence Desk

Conducts objective investigations, interviews witnesses regarding incidents, compiles comprehensive investigation findings, and provides early tracking to prevent behavioral misconduct.

CHAPTER FOUR: RIGHTS OF STUDENTS

  • Right to a Fair Hearing: No disciplinary sanction may be applied without a formal investigation and evidentiary hearing.
  • Right to Legal Representation: Access to representation by an appointed student advocate/lawyer during trials.
  • Right to Respectful Treatment: Absolute protection against abusive language or degrading punishment.

CHAPTER FIVE: DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Obey School Rules: Unconditionally obey all established parameters outlined in this framework.
  • Respect Authority Figures: Demonstrate absolute respect to teachers, administrative staff, and student leadership structures.
  • Consistent Academic Attendance: Attend all scheduled classes, preps, assemblies, and official institutional activities.

CHAPTER SIX: PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS & SANCTIONS

Section A: Prohibited Behaviors

  • Drug & Substance Abuse: Possession or consumption of alcohol, cigarettes, or narcotics. Sanction: Immediate suspension and probation.
  • Theft: Stealing personal or institutional properties. Sanction: Restitution and suspension.
  • Violence & Bullying: Fighting or harassment. Sanction: Manual work allocation and warning.
  • Unauthorized Electronics: Phones, smart gadgets, or radios. Sanction: Confiscation until returned to parents.

CHAPTER SEVEN: PREFECTORIAL CODE OF CONDUCT

This Leadership Charter defines the rules governing student leaders (prefects), religious leaders, dormitory leaders, and form captains appointed to assist the staff in ensuring efficient administration of the school.

  • The Head Prefect: Overall student leader and chief representative of the student body.
  • Deputy Head Prefect: Assists the Head Prefect directly and assumes authority in their absence.
  • Discipline Prefect & Deputy: Assists the staff directly in enforcing school rules across the campus.

CHAPTER EIGHT: UGANDA NATIONAL STUDENTS ASSOCIATION (UNSA)

Namungoona High School maintains full compliance with national student leadership frameworks. The UNSA branch represents national student integration interests inside our boundary walls.

CHAPTER NINE: JUDICIAL PROCESSES & TRIAL MECHANICS

Cases require clear factual verification by the Intelligence Desk and Prosecutor before trials can proceed. Accused students have the right to full defense parameters, cross-examination loops, and appeals through the office of the Headmaster.

CHAPTER TEN: COMMUNICATION RULES

All official notifications or briefings concerning the judicial court updates must pass explicitly through the authorized Court Spokesperson to avoid misinformation or campus rumors.

CHAPTER ELEVEN: AMENDMENTS & ENFORCEMENT

Structural alterations to this constitution require mutual approval from both the school administration board and the student council body to guarantee legitimacy.
